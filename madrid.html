📍 Route Overview

Your Camino Madrid journey begins in Spain's vibrant capital and winds through the historic heart of Castile, taking you through charming medieval towns, across ancient bridges, and through landscapes rich with pilgrimage tradition.

Highlights: The route passes through Segovia with its famous Roman aqueduct, medieval Coca with its impressive castle, and numerous small villages that have welcomed pilgrims for centuries. The path connects with the main Camino Francés route, continuing the ancient tradition of pilgrimage to Santiago de Compostela.

Best Time to Walk: April is an excellent choice for this route, offering mild weather and spring blooms while avoiding the intense summer heat of central Spain.
